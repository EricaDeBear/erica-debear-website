# Privacy Policy

**Effective Date:** January 1, 2025
**Governing Law:** California Consumer Privacy Act (CCPA/CPRA), CalOPPA

See [Your California Privacy Rights](#10-your-california-privacy-rights-ccpa--cpra) for full details, including the right to know, delete, correct, and opt out of the sale or sharing of your personal information.

## 1. Introduction

The Rise Group, operating under Real Brokerage Technologies, Inc. ("we," "us," or "our"), is committed to protecting your privacy. This Privacy Policy explains how we collect, use, disclose, and protect your personal information when you visit our website, submit inquiry forms, or otherwise interact with us.

## 2. Information We Collect

We may collect the following categories of personal information:

- **Identifiers:** Name, email address, postal address, phone number, IP address, and similar identifiers.
- **Real Estate Transaction Data:** Property preferences, search history on our Site, home-buying or selling status, and related real estate information you provide.
- **Log and Usage Data:** Browser type and version, pages visited, time and date of visits, referring/exit pages, clickstream data, and device information.
- **Communications:** Records of correspondence, including emails and phone calls with our agents.
- **Financial Information:** If you request a mortgage referral or valuation, we may collect limited financial information you voluntarily provide.
- **Cookie and Tracking Data:** Information collected automatically through cookies and similar technologies (see our [Cookie Policy](/legal/cookie-policy)).

## 3. How We Collect Information

We collect information:

1. Directly from you when you submit forms, create an account, or contact us;
2. Automatically as you navigate our Site through cookies and analytics tools; and
3. From third parties such as MLS data providers, marketing analytics vendors, and referral partners.

## 4. How We Use Your Information

We use the information we collect to:

- Provide real estate services and respond to your inquiries;
- Send you property listings, market updates, and other information you request;
- Contact you about our services, including via phone, email, and text message;
- Personalize your experience on our Site;
- Analyze Site usage and improve our services;
- Comply with legal obligations under California and federal law;
- Enforce our Terms of Use and protect our legal rights.

We will not sell or disclose your personal information to third parties for their own direct marketing purposes without your prior consent.

## 10. Your California Privacy Rights (CCPA / CPRA)

If you are a California resident, you have the following rights under the California Consumer Privacy Act (CCPA) and California Privacy Rights Act (CPRA):

| Right | Description |
|-------|-------------|
| **Right to Know** | Request disclosure of the categories and specific pieces of personal information we have collected about you, the sources of that information, the business purpose for collecting it, and the categories of third parties with whom we share it. |
| **Right to Delete** | Request that we delete personal information we have collected from you, subject to certain exceptions. |
| **Right to Correct** | Request correction of inaccurate personal information we maintain about you. |
| **Right to Opt Out of Sale or Sharing** | We do not sell personal information. If we ever do, you have the right to opt out. |
| **Right to Limit Use of Sensitive Personal Information** | Limit our use of sensitive personal information to what is necessary to perform the services you request. |
| **Right to Non-Discrimination** | We will not discriminate against you for exercising any of these rights. |

To exercise any of these rights, submit a verifiable consumer request to:

We will respond within **45 days** as required by California law.

## 11. "Do Not Track" and Global Privacy Control

California law (CalOPPA) requires us to disclose how we respond to "Do Not Track" (DNT) signals. We currently do not alter our data collection practices in response to DNT signals. However, we honor **Global Privacy Control (GPC)** signals where technically feasible, treating them as a valid opt-out request under CCPA/CPRA.
